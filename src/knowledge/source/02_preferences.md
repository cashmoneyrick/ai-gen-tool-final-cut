# Preferences

## Packaging mockups

Preference:
Keep the pouch premium, feminine, intentional, and tactile rather than generic or decorative.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
embroidered packaging mockups
Notes:
The ideal feel is small luxury packaging, not novelty merch.

Preference:
Use depth, layering, and tonal separation instead of just adding more objects.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
embroidered packaging mockups
Notes:
"More detailed" should mean stronger foreground, midground, background, overlap, and natural scene richness.

Preference:
Prefer harder crops and partial cutoffs when a scene starts feeling too complete.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
embroidered packaging mockups
Notes:
Subjects can be partially cropped by edges or seams to preserve the "stolen panel from a larger artwork" feel.

Preference:
Let artwork bleed organically around the pouch rather than sitting inside a strict square or patch.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
embroidered packaging mockups
Notes:
The scene should feel alive and naturally cut from a larger world.

Preference:
Keep the design realistically makeable in embroidery.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
embroidered packaging mockups
Notes:
Readable motifs beat intricate over-detail.

Preference:
Hide YPS as an easter egg inside the scene logic.
Strength: medium
Applies to:
embroidered packaging mockups
Notes:
Preferred when it can be done subtly without forcing it.

Preference:
Use richer scenes through overlap and world-building, but avoid flat symmetry and staged badge layouts.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
embroidered packaging mockups
Notes:
The scene should feel authored, not template-like.

Preference:
Maintain continuity across pouch material, angle, background, and general product-photo setup.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
embroidered packaging mockups
Notes:
Continuity matters almost as much as the scene itself.

## Nail flatlays

Preference:
Default flatlay format is five loose nails in one straight horizontal row on pure white.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
nail flatlays
Notes:
Head-on, evenly spaced, camera perpendicular, minimal shadow, anti-glare.

Preference:
Keep the nails handmade and believable, not machine-perfect.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
flatlays, product shots, reverse-engineered nails
Notes:
Slight asymmetry and realistic gel thickness help.

Preference:
Preserve exact design fidelity from the source instead of "improving" the art.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
flatlays generated from existing sets
Notes:
The task is faithful translation, not reinterpretation.

## Hand shots

Preference:
Use image references whenever possible.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
on-hand lifestyle shots, reverse engineering
Notes:
Reference images massively improve fidelity.

Preference:
When using two references, make the design reference the hard source of truth and the pose reference secondary.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
on-hand lifestyle shots
Notes:
This should be stated explicitly in the prompt.

Preference:
For lifestyle hand shots, realism and candid believability often beat studio perfection.
Strength: medium
Applies to:
real-life hand photos, review-style images, casual content
Notes:
Natural lighting, believable skin texture, and slight imperfection improve authenticity.

## Cateye sets

Preference:
Use Wide Cat Eye as the default house pattern.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
cateye collection
Notes:
Only break from it if a month clearly looks bad.

Preference:
Use the three-layer stack: black base, silver magnetic layer, translucent color cover.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
cateye collection
Notes:
This is the preferred system for color continuity and magnetic life.

Preference:
Keep the magnetic field broad, filled, and internal with darker edges.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
cateye collection
Notes:
Avoid circular, halo, donut, or hotspot reads.

Preference:
Keep the surface reflection tiny, soft, and subordinate to the magnetic effect.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
cateye collection
Notes:
One short soft dash highlight near the upper center is preferred.

Preference:
Lock the camera, shape, framing, and highlight behavior across months, then only swap the birthstone color behavior.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
cateye collection
Notes:
Continuity first, color variation second.

## Prompt formatting

Preference:
Use structured JSON Bucket Method for image prompts.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
current image generation prompt writing
Notes:
Buckets are Subject, Style, Lighting, Composition, and Technical Refinements.

Preference:
Use professional visual language when helpful, especially for nails.
Strength: medium
Applies to:
nail prompts, product shots
Notes:
Useful terms include anisotropy, iridescence, refraction, subsurface scattering, anti-glare, 50mm macro logic, and controlled highlight rolloff.

Preference:
Conflict exists on packaging prompt format.
Strength: medium
Applies to:
packaging prompts
Notes:
Older packaging threads preferred one plain-text finished prompt with no markdown formatting. Later system-level preference shifted toward structured JSON Bucket Method for image prompts. Current best interpretation: default to JSON Bucket Method for new image prompts unless continuity with older packaging threads specifically calls for the plain-text packaging style.

## Use of references

Preference:
Proactively ask whether reference images are available.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
all image workflows
Notes:
References materially improve fidelity and reduce drift.

Preference:
Promote approved generations into the reference chain.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
iterative workflows
Notes:
Once a generation is approved, use it as the new anchor for future edits.

Preference:
Use one design reference plus one pose/scene reference when translating a set into a new format.
Strength: strong
Applies to:
reverse engineering, hand shots, format conversion
Notes:
This is the preferred reference handling pattern.
